The Genesis of a Naming Philosophy

IKEA's unique naming system wasn't a marketing ploy. It was a practical solution born from founder Ingvar Kamprad's dyslexia, making it difficult to work with standard numerical item codes.

Deconstructing the Naming Convention

The modern system is a highly structured taxonomy. Each product category is systematically assigned names from a specific, curated group of words, creating a powerful link between name and function.

Primary Sources of Names

The name bank is dominated by Scandinavian proper nouns, primarily geographical locations and people's names, reinforcing the brand's identity.

The Rule Breakers

The system's rigidity is intentionally broken for marketing value or functional clarity. These are not errors, but deliberate strategic choices.

BILLY

CATEGORY: Bookcase

Expected Rule: Profession or Scandinavian Boy's Name.

Actual Rationale: A Personal/Story exception, named in honor of IKEA employee Billy Likjedhal.

KROSSA

CATEGORY: Spice Mill

Expected Rule: Fish, Mushroom, or Adjective.

Actual Rationale: A Functional exception, from the Swedish verb "to crush."

LACK

CATEGORY: Living Room Furniture

Expected Rule: Swedish Place Name.

Actual Rationale: A Functional exception, from the Swedish noun for "lacquer."

Global Challenges & Linguistic Blunders

Applying a Swedish-centric system globally creates noise. Names can have unintended meanings in other languages, forcing regional changes and creating data inconsistencies.

From Taxonomy to Technology

The rule-based system is ideal for a machine learning project. A hierarchical model can be trained to first classify the name *type*, then predict the final product category.
